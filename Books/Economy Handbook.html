Economy Handbook
Lords, Rich Patrons, Collectors
If you are in possession of an extremely valuable item in need of sale, the only people that are likely to be able
to afford such things are the nobles, the richest merchants, and wealthy sorcerers and priests. If you find
yourself in possession of an extremely rare and expensive object, a rich patron will be the only way to sell it for
near it's true value.
If there are no craftsmen or market to call upon, you may attempt to solicit an item from the common folks of a
town by randomly advertising. The GM will determine if the item you are looking for is available, but this will
take time and will have a low probability of success. Even still, if you manage to trade goods in this kind of
environment, this is where the best deals can be made. Unfortunately, such an environment tends to be low on
coin, so there is a hard limit on the volume of goods sold or purchased.
The world of Thule is a collection of relatively small tribes, city states, and occasion Empires. Most locations do
not have sprawling markets with a wide availability of things for sale. Likewise, selling your own gear can be
difficult. There are several situations in which you can attempt to sell or buy items.
The Market Place
The larger settlements in Thule typically grant access to a large marketplace. This is a location where a wide
variety of items can be bought and sold. When trying to buy an item in the marketplace, the GM will make a
determination of whether the item will be available there. The larger the marketplace, the more likely items will
be available for sale. Rare items might still be unavailable even in the largest of marketplaces. One should
beware though, marketplaces are typically filled with cutthroat merchants with lofty dreams of riches. Prices
tend to be higher. If you wish to sell items in a marketplace, you can set up shop with the blessing of the local
lord. There tends to be a small 1% tax on items sold , though some lords may have higher taxes.

Commodity Traders
Some rich lords are willing to purchase or sell large quantities of raw materials such as lumber, grains, metal, etc.
You can find such lords in many towns and this is a useful way of generating wealth by moving such materials
around as a merchant.
Storage Pits, Souterrains, Vaults
Local Lords are often resposible for making sure that the proper amount of supplies, weapons, and general
wealth has been collected to provide for the people. If these stores are in need of refilling, this can be a location
to sell useful equipment and supplies.
Thoughout Thule there are many fine craftsmen who are capable of crafting a wide variety of goods. Craftsmen
usually have cheaper prices than merchants, but will not be able to provide nearly the same variety. Finding a
good and reliable craftsmen is a boon for any buyer looking for quality goods at a low price. Craftsmen tend to
be anywhere from level 6-10 in crafting. Higher level craftsmen tend to become famous for the quality of their
work. Usually craftsmen are much more eager to sell than to buy. Craftsmen will buy the kinds of items that they
sell at a steep discount. Craftsmen also tend to accept goods (especially related to the craft) as payment for
their wares.

Proving grounds are places where warriors meet and fight each other. An appropriate place for
dueling, training, and other activities. Warriors can improve their relations with tribes by defeating
other warriors in their proving grounds in fights to the blood, or wandscuffing. Defeat an Elite to
gain a rank of respect. The proving grounds is also a good place for making bets.
The meeting grounds are where tribesmen gather to socialize and seek work. 2d6 Mercenaries or
workers can be found here 50% of the time. Increase this likelihood by 10% if you have a good
relationship, 20% for excellent, or 60% if beloved. The meeting grounds can be checked every day,
but the chance of finding what you are looking for decreases by 10% each consecutive day to a
minimum of 10%.

Non-Combat Hirelings
Guards expect to have to fight defensively when random threats arise. They have not signed up to go on the
offensive and will not take any challenge that their hirer himself is unwilling to take. They will not attempt to
defend their hirer from foes that they are clearly unable to defeat. Their hirer may accuse them of cowardice (A
criminal offense) if they retreat from a foe, and a tribunal of his peers will decide whether or not retreat
constitutes a criminal action. Falsely accusing a guard of cowardice will result in losing one rank of relationship
with the tribe. If guards enter into two or more serious combats in a 28 day period on your behalf, they will ask
for Skjegeld (Shield Money) , which is a one time (Monthly) bonus payment made for dangerous work. If
Skjegeld is not paid, the guard will abandon you and you will lose one rank of reputation with their tribe.
Mercenaries
Mercenaries
Mercenaries will go into serious danger (though they will still not take actions certain to result in their own
death. They understand that they will be putting their lives on the line against dangerous foes. Mercenaries
expect not be used in sacrificial ways, and they will charge blood money (Krigeld) for members of their force
that die in combat. Failure to pay Krigeld is a criminal offense, punishable by six months of outlawry and
forcibly acquisition of the assets of the guilty. Additionally, your relationship with that tribe will change to
hostile. Many times, mercenaries are put on reserve. While this is going on, their patron will pay them a small
fee to be on standby whenever needed. Once they are called into action, their mercenary fee begins to be
charged.
Non-Combat hirelings do not expect to die when they are doing your work. If they do, you are expected to pay
weregeld to their families. Failure to do so will result in your relationship with the tribe falling by one.
